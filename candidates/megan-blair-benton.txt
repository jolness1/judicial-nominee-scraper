On November 14, 2025, President Trump nominated Megan Blair Benton to the United States District Court for the Western District of Missouri. She was confirmed on February 4, 2026.


Biography

Born in Columbia, Missouri in 1985, Benton studied at Lincoln University (2004) before she earned her B.A. and M.A., both in political science, from Emory University in 2007. She earned her J.D. from Vanderbilt University in 2010.


Legal Experience

Benton began her legal career as a summer law clerk at Bryan Cave Leighton Paisner LLP in 2008 and 2009. After law school, she clerked for Judges Ralph W. Dau and Barbara M. Scheper at the Los Angeles County Superior Court (2011–2012).

She then joined The Kuhlman Law Firm (2012–2014) before serving as an Assistant Prosecuting Attorney in the Platte County Prosecutor’s Office (2014–2017; 2019–2021). Benton later joined the Sixth Judicial Circuit of Platte County, Missouri, serving first as an Associate Circuit Judge (2021–2023) and currently as a Circuit Judge (2023–present).
